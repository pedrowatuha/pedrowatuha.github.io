About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at the Getulio Vargas Foundation (FGV), currently visiting the University of Pittsburgh hosted by Professor Mahrad Sharifvaghefi. My Ph.D. supervisor is Professor Marcelo Moreira.

My research lies at the intersection of econometrics, causal inference, and machine learning, with applications to finance and macroeconomics. I am particularly interested in weak identification, generative approaches to variable selection, and large language model methods applied to economic measurement.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I completed my Bachelor's degree in Economics at the University of Brasilia, where I also worked as a researcher at the Laboratory of Machine Learning (LAMFO) and supervised Professional Master's students on machine learning applications in finance.
